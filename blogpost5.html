Conclusion

  • By Hannah Kwan
  • March 10, 2020

In a survey of HCI research on aesthetics, due to the subjective nature of aesthetics, studies can be variant. The relationship between aesthetics and usability is hard to quantify and much of the research in this area is also based on the opinions of the participants. However, some of these studies are more than a decade ago, so in the future, there may be studies that can more accurately capture the user’s perception of aesthetics.

While research can be conflicting, it is hard to deny that aesthetics have become an essential part of mobile applications. As such, companies have published design guidelines for developers to follow to promote consistency among systems along with do’s and don’t’s for general usability. This demonstrates that there is some economic value in designing good experiences for the user. With this background, developers should consider the aesthetic design of their applications, since there is research that it increases the credibility of the application and there are economic implications.
